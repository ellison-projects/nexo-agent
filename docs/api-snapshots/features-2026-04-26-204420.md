# NexoPRM — Product Features

_Last reviewed: 2026-04-18 · Last updated: 2026-04-26 (project notes gained per-note tags for slicing a long timeline)_




A living, short-form summary of every user-facing feature in NexoPRM. Each entry explains the feature's purpose in plain language (not how it's built) and ends with a **Usage** tag that reflects how much I actually rely on it today.

Keep this file up to date whenever a significant feature ships, a feature's purpose meaningfully changes, or a feature's usage tier shifts. Bump **Last updated** on any edit; bump **Last reviewed** only after walking through every entry to reconfirm it still matches reality. This is the single source of truth for:

- Humans browsing the repo
- Claude Code (referenced from the root `CLAUDE.md`)
- AI agents via `GET /api/agent/features`

The four pillars (see `CLAUDE.md` for the North Star) are **family**, **relationships**, **health**, and **ambitions**. Family is the anchor. Many features cut across pillars; they're listed where they fit best.

## Usage tags

- **Core** — used regularly, load-bearing
- **Occasional** — useful but only some weeks or situations
- **Beta** — newer feature, haven't really used yet
- **Aspirational** — added hoping to use more, not really used today
- **Needs refining** — valuable, but the build state isn't dialed in yet
- **On the chopping block** — considering changing or removing

## People & Relationships

### People
A searchable directory of the humans that matter in my life — family, friends, coworkers, community. Every person has a profile that acts as a cheat sheet for the next time I see them: what they care about, what's going on in their life, what I've talked about before.
_Usage: Core_

### Connection Groups
Groups people into real-world units I already think about as a unit — a couple, a family, a team — so I can capture shared context against the group instead of fragmenting it across individuals. Mostly used for family groupings today. Also the way I **link** people together — saying "link Sam to John" or "link Eli's friend" means adding them to an existing connection group or creating a new one; there is no separate pairwise link concept. Each member can carry an optional free-form role ("spouse", "child", "parent") so a family group reads as a family instead of a flat list of names.

Use a connection group (not a list) when I'd want to attach shared notes, shared important dates, or per-member roles to the collection. Families, couples, and close friend units belong here.
_Usage: Occasional_

### Lists
Lightweight named collections of people for specific **situations or actions** — "Active babysitters", "Christmas cards", "Kids' university friends", "People to invite to the barbecue". Optimized for pulling up the right roster in the moment: scan, pick a name, get on with it. No shared notes, no shared dates, no per-member roles. The Lists screen also acts as the hub for Groceries and Home Items.

Use a list (not a connection group) when the collection is purely a roster I'd scan or filter by. If I ever want to attach shared context to the whole collection, it should be a connection group instead.
_Usage: Core_

### Connection Type
Each person is tagged as a Primary or Secondary connection — Primary for people I actively stay in touch with, Secondary for people I want to keep on my radar without actively maintaining. A lightweight attention-priority tag.
_Usage: On the chopping block_

## Capturing Context

### Moments
Timestamped notes about what happened with someone — a conversation, a milestone, a health update, something funny a kid said. Moments are the raw material of memory: they add up into a record I can revisit.
_Usage: Core_

### Things to Remember
Durable facts about a person that don't change often — allergies, kids' names, favorite coffee order, career goal. The stuff I'd blank on under pressure but wish I hadn't.
_Usage: Core_

### Important Dates
Recurring yearly dates that matter — birthdays, anniversaries, loss days, milestones — surfaced before they arrive so I don't find out from Facebook.
_Usage: Core_

### Working Notes
My weekly scratchpad. It starts as a brain dump of everything on my mind — priorities, thoughts I'm chewing on, decisions I'm working through — and then evolves into a working todo of major and minor tasks I need to get done, with items I check off as I finish them. I keep one active working note at a time (the latest), and it typically lives about a week before I clear it and start a new one. Older ones become snapshots in time I can flip back through to see how my thinking has evolved. Any heading (a project grouping) can be opened on its own dedicated page that shows just its child items and a big notes textarea for long-form project context — a way to zero in on one thing without the rest of the note in the way.
_Usage: Core_

### Areas of Focus
A short, editable list of the 3–5 things I'm actively prioritizing this season of life. Acts as the lens I use when deciding what deserves attention and what doesn't.
_Usage: Occasional_

### Trigger List
A custom curated list of prompts or themes I want to keep returning to — things that kick me out of autopilot and back into intention.
_Usage: Occasional_

## Follow-Through

### AI Reminders
The follow-through layer of the app — a single feed of things I need to circle back on, independent of any one pillar. A reminder is a short message (title), optional long-form notes for extra details, a due date, and an optional person and/or project. Reminders can come from three places:

- **Moments (automatic)** — the analyzer picks out implicit follow-ups ("should check in after the surgery", "ask how the interview went") and creates a reminder with a suggested due date and message.
- **Working-note todos (automatic)** — extracts time/date cues from individual items ("call Mom Friday", "by next Tuesday") and schedules a reminder for the right moment, applying a sensible lead time for deadlines.
- **Manual (agent- or project-created)** — the AI agent creates a one-off reminder directly via the agent API when I say "remind me to call the vet Friday" and there's no relationship context worth logging as a moment. The same path is exposed on the project detail page so dated tasks inside a multi-week thread (Mother's Day planning, interview prep) get nudges that show up both in the project and in the main reminders feed. These live in the same feed as the automatic ones, no `moment_id` attached.

The automatic flows share a content-hash dedup layer so each piece of text is analyzed once, and edits automatically re-queue the item. The framework is generic — new analyzable objects plug in by declaring a `feature` + `subject_type` and a candidate query. The entire feed is browsable at `/reminders` (linked from Settings), grouped by when things are due, and surfaced in the daily digest email. The idea is that passive capture and quick agent-speak both become active follow-through without me setting anything up manually.
_Usage: Core_

### Email Notifications
A handful of separate emails that keep me informed without opening the app: AI reminder digest (daily), important-date reminders for birthdays and anniversaries happening today or tomorrow (daily), a "Big Moments" summary of upcoming events in the next 30 days (Sundays), and todo-item reminders from working notes. For the mornings I don't open the app.
_Usage: Needs refining_

### Briefing (Agent-Facing)
A single consolidated snapshot covering the next 14 days forward and the last 7 days backward across every pillar, designed for an assistant agent to ground on in one call. Surfaces both what's coming up / still open and what I've finished recently (with timestamps) so an assistant can summarize the week and plan what's left. Powers external automations that help me stay on top of things.
_Usage: Core_

## Projects

### Projects
A home for multi-week initiatives — the running threads life comes in rather than single events. Each project is a lightweight container for an ongoing effort: a job search, Mother's Day planning, a half-marathon training plan, a kid's tough semester, a career pivot. Has a short description tagline, a longer free-form **overview notes** field (what the project is about — goals, background, context, the big-picture block you'd want to reread when you open the project), an optional pillar, an optional target date, optional people links, freeform tags, a dated collection of timeline **notes**, a flat list of **next actions**, and dated **reminders** (the project's own slice of the AI reminder feed — handy for "order flowers on April 30th" inside a Mother's Day project).

Each note has a `kind` — `note` (observation in the moment, the default) or `reflection` (distilled learning, "what I'd do differently"). Notes are fully editable — jot something down on Monday, come back Wednesday and rework it into a cleaner thought. Filter to "Reflections only" a year later and get the lessons in 30 seconds. Project-level tags (`mothers-day`, `half-marathon`, `interview-prep`) let recurring themes show up side-by-side across time. **Per-note tags** slice a single project's timeline — for a perpetual project like a yearly father-son trip, tagging each cycle's notes with the year (`2026`, `2027`) lets me filter to one cycle without splitting into multiple projects. The tag pool is per project; the filter bar appears at the top of the timeline once the project has at least one tagged note.

Deliberately not a task manager: one flat checklist per project, no stages, no priorities, no dependencies. Deliberately no close button either — stale projects sink into a "Quiet" band automatically; a soft-archive escape hatch exists for true wrap-up.
_Usage: Beta_

## Life Admin

### Recurring Tasks
The home for recurring life admin that doesn't belong anywhere else — the stuff that comes around on a cadence but isn't emotional (Important Dates) and isn't reactive (Home Maintenance). Each item has a flexible cadence (every N days, weeks, months, or years), an optional time of day ("7pm every Thursday"), a next-due timestamp that auto-advances when I mark it done, a lead time that controls when reminders start, and an escalate-when-overdue flag that decides whether a missed cycle stays surfaced (insurance, dentist) or quietly rolls forward (trash, HVAC filter). All-day items show up in the daily digest email; timed items also get an at-time email ping the moment they come due. ICS feed integration means I don't have to open the app to remember.

**Belongs here:** trash day, recycling, auto insurance renewal, HVAC filter, dentist every 6 months, annual physical, passport renewal, car inspection, quarterly estimated taxes.

**Does not belong here:** birthdays and anniversaries (→ Important Dates), "fix the leaky faucet" / "call roofer about the leak" (→ Home Maintenance, active-attention list), one-off todos for this week (→ Working Notes).
_Usage: Beta_

## Food & Home

### Meals
A personal library of meals I actually cook, tagged and searchable, so "what's for dinner?" has an answer instead of a stare at the fridge.
_Usage: Beta_

### Meal Plans
A rough weekly plan of what I'm cooking when. Not a rigid schedule — just enough structure to shop and prep with intent.
_Usage: Beta_

### Food Log
Lightweight logging of what I (or the family) ate, for health awareness without turning into a calorie-tracking app.
_Usage: Beta_

### Groceries
An active running shopping list with items grouped by category. Built for the aisle — tap to check off, tap to add what I just noticed I'm out of. New items land at the top of the list so the freshest thing I just thought of is front and centre. Tap an item to open its details (name, note, photos) — photos (e.g. a specific brand) also show a small badge on the row that opens a swipeable gallery for quick recognition in the store. As I type in "Add item…", the suggestions dropdown surfaces matching Stash entries tagged `grocery` alongside my history — picking a Stash suggestion snapshots its title and links the row back to the stash entry, so its photo shows inline and tapping the thumbnail opens the full Stash detail for brand context in the aisle.
_Usage: Core_

### Home Items
The things around my house worth remembering details about — appliances, tools, furniture — with warranty info, repair notes, model numbers. The info I need the exact moment something breaks.
_Usage: Needs refining_

### Stash
A pocket knowledge base for the stray facts that don't belong to a person — a brand of floss I liked, a cafe with good salads, the gate code at my brother's place, a book quote I want again. Each entry has a title, an optional long-form note, optional photos (for recognition in the store), an optional free-text location, and loose tags like "healthy" or "kids love it". Tap into it when you're standing in the aisle asking "what was that one?" — search by title/note/tag or filter by a single tag chip. Counterpart to Things to Remember, which is person-anchored.
_Usage: Beta_

## Platform

### AI Agent API
A separate, key-authenticated HTTP surface (`/api/agent/*`) that lets an external agent or script read and write every resource on my behalf — including attaching images and files by URL. This is what lets Claude or other assistants actually help me stay on top of my life rather than just talking about it.

Beyond plain CRUD, the surface also exposes purpose-built **read-only views** so an agent doesn't have to chain a dozen queries to ground itself:

- **Briefing** (`/api/agent/briefing`) — a single consolidated snapshot of the next 14 days and the last 7, across every pillar.
- **Features** (`/api/agent/features`) — this file itself, so the agent knows what the app can do before suggesting anything.
- **Reports** (`/api/agent/reports/*`) — narrow rollups that answer "give me the list of people who match X", e.g. people grouped by connection type (primary / secondary / uncategorized) to surface gaps.

_Usage: Core_
